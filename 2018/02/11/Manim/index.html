Story behind Manim

About

It’s not that I want to discourage others from doing similar things, quite the contrary, but often my workflow and development with manim can make it more difficult for an outsider to learn than other better-documented animation tools.

Now you know manim is a poorly documented tool. This will be a killing problem if you want to try it out. Meanwhile, you know how this kind of animation is produced, for example, using a python library.

It enforces a uniqueness of style, for example, which is by its very nature a benefit that can’t be shared.

So cool! 😏

I was fortunate enough to be able to start forging a less traditional path into math outreach thanks to Khan Academy’s talent search, which led me to make content for them in 2015/2016 as their multivariable calculus fellow. I still contribute to Khan Academy every now and then, as I live near enough and we remain friendly, but my full time these days is devoted to 3blue1brown.

What resources did you use to get the mathematical knowledge you have today?

I’m still always learning, and moreover always trying to refine how I learn, and I have no clear answers on what is best. So take anything I say with the knowledge that it should be heavily supplemented with advice that other (wiser) minds have to offer.

In general, I do think the best way to learn is to emphasize solving problems, rather than reading/watching alone. Math is fundamentally about patterns, and solving problems is a good forcing function for immersing yourself in patterns.

Update

Criticizing the present situation without giving a better solution is stupid, you should know how the situation becomes this, the reason why the current plan is activating.

I knew that if the available tools can’t express your mind, invent a new tool.

It’s been more than one year since I wrote this post, as you can see, there have many improvements, many people have involved in this tool, there are question posted on SO labeled with [manim], there are video tutorials on how to use manim on youtube, there are rather complete hands-on introductions, there are clear ways installation methods adapt to various scenes.

Yet the core problems I think is not about learning the cool animation effects skill, but the explanation on the concept from Mathematics. The preparation for each video, there is a script, you can’t produce a nearly flawless work without loads of practices. And this rule applied to every occasion.

From the author’s past experience, I think there are very few people who can do this work as good as him. He is into fundamental mathematics and he is good at it, he is into online education?
